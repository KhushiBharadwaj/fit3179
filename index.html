Most university students in Australia are domestic (around 76%), while international students account for roughly a quarter. This highlights the global pull of Australia’s education sector, with international enrolments forming a vital part of campus diversity and funding.

Indigenous students remain a small minority across higher education within the domestic population, though their presence is strongest in Society & Culture and Education. Engineering, IT, and Architecture continue to show the lowest Indigenous participation, underscoring ongoing equity gaps in technical disciplines.

Male participation peaks in the Australian Captial Territory (16.7%) and New South Wales (15.8%), reflecting strong engagement in states with higher STEM enrolments. Tasmania records the lowest male rate at 14%, showing limited higher education participation in smaller regional economies.

Women lead in higher education participation nationwide, with the Australian Capital Territory recording the highest share (19.1%) and Tasmania the lowest (14.5%). The consistent state-level advantage highlights women’s growing presence in tertiary learning across Australia.

Health and Education fields are predominantly female, while Engineering and IT remain male-dominated. Creative Arts and Natural Sciences are the most balanced disciplines, showing gradual progress toward gender diversity across study fields.
